Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a popular and hearty North Indian dish, especially loved in the Punjab region and Delhi. It consists of spicy and flavorful chickpea curry (Chole) served alongside deep-fried, fluffy bread (Bhature) made from maida (all-purpose flour). It is often enjoyed for breakfast or lunch, but can be a fulfilling meal any time of the day

Key Characteristics

  • Hearty and Filling:Chole Bhature is a substantial meal due to the combination of rich chickpea curry and fried bread.
  • Spicy and Tangy Flavors:The chole are typically flavored with a blend of spices, onion, tomato, ginger, garlic, and sometimes incorporate ingredients like dried pomegranate seeds (anardana) or amla for a tangy element.
  • Fluffy and Crispy Bhature:The bhature are deep-fried, resulting in a bread that is crispy on the outside and soft and airy inside.
  • Served with Accompaniments:Chole Bhature is often accompanied by pickled onions, green chutney, and sometimes a glass of cooling Lassi or Masala Aloo.

Basic steps to prepare

Chole

  1. Soak Chickpeas:Wash and soak the chickpeas overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  2. Boil Chickpeas:In a pressure cooker, combine the soaked chickpeas, water, salt, whole spices (like black cardamom, cloves, and bay leaves), and optionally a tea bag for color. Pressure cook until the chickpeas are soft and tender. Discard the whole spices and tea bag.
  3. Prepare the Gravy:Heat oil in a pan or kadai. Saut√© chopped onions and ginger-garlic paste until the onions are translucent. Add chopped tomatoes, salt, and cook until they soften and the oil begins to separate.
  4. Add Spices:Incorporate a blend of spices like coriander powder, chana masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and optionally dried pomegranate powder (anardana) or amla powder. Cook the spices for a minute or two until fragrant.
  5. Combine and Simmer:Add the boiled chickpeas (along with some of the reserved cooking water) to the gravy. Mix well, cover, and simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Adjust salt and add garam masala to finish.
  6. Garnish:Garnish with fresh cilantro.

Bhature

  1. Prepare the Dough:Sift together all-purpose flour (maida), baking powder, and baking soda. Combine with yogurt, salt, sugar, and water, kneading into a soft dough.
  2. Rest the Dough:Lightly oil the dough, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rest for at least 30 minutes, or longer for better results.
  3. Roll and Fry:Divide the dough into balls and roll each ball into an oval or round shape, as thin as possible without tearing. Heat oil in a kadai or wok for deep-frying. Drop the flattened dough into the hot oil. It should puff up almost immediately.
  4. Cook and Serve:Fry until both sides are golden brown. Remove from oil and place on paper towels to drain excess oil. Serve hot with the prepared Chole.
