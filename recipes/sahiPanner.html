Sahi Panner

Description

Shahi Paneer is a creamy Indian curry made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a rich gravy of cream, tomatoes, onions, cashews and a blend of aromatic spices. The word "Shahi" means royal, highlighting the dish's rich taste and association with regal cuisine, originating in the Mughal era.

Key characteristics

  • Rich, creamy gravy: The gravy is made with cream, pureed tomatoes, onions, ginger-garlic paste, cashews, and sometimes almonds or yogurt for a smoother texture.
  • Mildly sweet and less spicy: Compared to other paneer curries like Kadai Paneer or Paneer Lababdar, Shahi Paneer is generally sweeter and less spicy, appealing to a wider range of palates.
  • Aromatic spices: Cardamom, saffron, cinnamon, cloves, cumin seeds, ginger, garlic, coriander powder, and garam masala are commonly used to create a fragrant and flavorful dish without being overly spicy.
  • Paneer cubes: Cubes of paneer are gently simmered in the rich gravy, absorbing the flavors and becoming tender.

Basic steps to prepare

While recipes vary slightly, the general process involves:

  2. Adding Spices:Incorporating spices like turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, cardamom, and saffron.
  3. Simmering with Paneer:Adding paneer cubes to the gravy and simmering until the flavors meld.
  4. Finishing Touches:Garnish with fresh cream, coriander leaves, and sometimes saffron strands.
